What is microblading?

Every few hours after the appointment, make sure to blot with a clean tissue or paper towel to remove excess lymph and blood particles to prevent scabbing.

How long does the process take?

For the first microblading session, the entire procedure should take about 2.5 - 3 hours. During this session, the brow artist will measure and shape the clientâ€™s brows, then check with the client for approval. Then, the pigment color is selected and agreed upon with the client. Next, your brows are numbed for about 10 - 15 minutes before the procedure begins. For more detailed steps on the procedure, click here.

The second touch-up microblading session takes place after 4-6 weeks after the initial session, as a "perfection" session to address any discrepancies with shape, color, or any other minor issues. This session will take approximately 1.5 - 2 hours to complete.

How long does it take to heal?

It takes about 14 days for the basic healing to be over, and about a month for the brows and color to go completely back to normal. After 4-6 weeks the client can come back for the touch-up session. For more details on the healing and aftercare process please click here.

Does it hurt?

Of course everyone's pain tolerance is different, but generally speaking the pain level is mild to none. I personally consider myself to have a low pain tolerance and it was not a problem for me at all! Before any needles touch the skin, a topical anesthetic will be applied to the area for 10 - 15 minutes to numb the area. Anesthetics may contain lidocaine, prilocaine, benzocaine, tetracaine and epinephrine. A little redness and swelling in the area after the procedure is normal.

Is the touch-up necessary?

After the first 14 days you can resume most of your normal activities. For the most long lasting results, continue avoiding things like chemical peels and sun exposure (use sunscreen!) to the brow area. Anything that damages or removes the skin will also damage or remove the pigment in that skin and cause it to fade faster.

How long does it last?

Microblading typically lasts 1 - 3 years, depending on many factors. Despite being called "cosmetic tattoos", microblading pigments are not implanted as deeply as traditional tattoos (if they are, they can turn blue or red and need color correction!). The client's skin type, skincare routine, and sun exposure will all have an effect on color retention. See more aftercare tips to enhance longevity here.

What are the pre and post care procedures?

Prior to your procedure, clients are advised to do the following:

  • Avoid alcohol, aspirin, ibuprofen, caffeine, and any other blood thinners for 24 hours
  • Avoid tanning
  • Avoid chemicals or harsh exfoliants for two weeks prior
  • Please DO come in with your brow makeup so the artist can see your preferred brow shape. Reference pictures of the brow shape and color you desire are also good to bring.

For aftercare procedures, please visit the aftercare page.

Who is not a suitable candidate for permanent makeup?

If you fall into any of the below categories, you CANNOT receive a permanent makeup procedure:

  • Pregnant or breast-feeding
  • Received Botox or fillers in the last 2 weeks
  • Received a chemical peel, deep tan, or sunburn in the last 2 weeks
  • Have used Accutane within the past 12 months
  • Have diabetes
  • Have heart conditions or use heart medications
  • Skin disease in the area where you want the procedure done
  • Any other serious medical condition that may require a doctor's note

These rules are for the client's safety, and are required by the insurance company. The client will need to sign a waiver before the procedure stating that none of these scenarios apply.

I also reserve the right to refuse service to anyone who I deem not suitable for this procedure, or if the client's condition lies beyond my level of experience to address appropriately.

What are the risks and complications?

All risks and complications are minimized by state and county law mandating licensing of the permanent makeup artist, routine inspection of the permanent makeup facility, and requiring annual blood borne pathogen training. All procedures are conducted with sterile, one-time use disposable instruments.

Although allergic reaction to pigments are rare, it could be possible for some clients. We suggest a pigment patch test 5 - 7 days prior to the procedure for anyone with high skin sensitivities and allergies, particularly involving:

  • Makeup products
  • Gold, silver, nickel
  • Hair dyes
